Name: Everest Base Camp Trek
Duration: 12-14 days
Difficulty: Hard
Max Altitude: 5,545m (Kala Patthar)
Best Season: March-May, September-November
Permits: TIMS Card, Sagarmatha National Park Permit
Cost: $1,200-$2,000
Description: The most famous trek in the world. Fly to Lukla, trek through Sherpa villages, reach Everest Base Camp and Kala Patthar for views of Mount Everest.
Tip: Spend an extra acclimatization day in Namche Bazaar.

Name: Annapurna Circuit Trek
Duration: 15-18 days
Difficulty: Moderate to Hard
Max Altitude: 5,416m (Thorong La Pass)
Best Season: March-May, September-November
Permits: TIMS Card, ACAP Permit
Cost: $1,000-$1,800
Description: Classic trek circling the Annapurna massif. Diverse landscapes from subtropical forests to high desert. Cross the famous Thorong La Pass.
Tip: Take your time ascending to avoid altitude sickness at Thorong La.

Name: Ghorepani Poon Hill Trek
Duration: 4-5 days
Difficulty: Easy to Moderate
Max Altitude: 3,210m (Poon Hill)
Best Season: March-May, September-December
Permits: TIMS Card, ACAP Permit
Cost: $300-$600
Description: Short and rewarding trek perfect for beginners. Famous for stunning sunrise views over Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges from Poon Hill.
Tip: Wake up at 4:30 AM for the sunrise hike to Poon Hill viewpoint.

Name: Langtang Valley Trek
Duration: 7-9 days
Difficulty: Moderate
Max Altitude: 3,870m (Kyanjin Ri)
Best Season: March-May, September-November
Permits: TIMS Card, Langtang National Park Permit
Cost: $500-$900
Description: Less crowded trek near Kathmandu. Beautiful valley with Tamang culture, glaciers, and mountain views. Recovering well after 2015 earthquake.
Tip: Visit the local cheese factory in Kyanjin Gompa.

Name: Manaslu Circuit Trek
Duration: 14-16 days
Difficulty: Hard
Max Altitude: 5,160m (Larkya La Pass)
Best Season: March-May, September-November
Permits: Restricted Area Permit, MCAP, ACAP
Cost: $1,500-$2,500
Description: Remote and challenging trek around Mount Manaslu (8,163m). Less touristy alternative to Annapurna Circuit with pristine nature and Tibetan culture.
Tip: Must trek with a registered guide and at least one other trekker.
