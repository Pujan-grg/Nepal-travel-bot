Name: Dashain
Date: September-October (15 days)
Type: Religious
Description: Nepal's biggest and longest festival celebrating goddess Durga's victory. Families reunite, fly kites, play on bamboo swings. Government offices and many businesses close.
Tip: Expect crowded transportation and closed shops. Book flights well in advance.

Name: Tihar (Deepawali)
Date: October-November (5 days)
Type: Religious/Cultural
Description: Festival of lights similar to Diwali. Each day honors different animals (crows, dogs, cows). Laxmi Puja is main day with oil lamps and fireworks. Brothers receive tika from sisters on Bhai Tika.
Tip: See beautiful rangoli designs and oil lamps decorating homes in Kathmandu.

Name: Holi (Fagu Purnima)
Date: March (Full moon day)
Type: Cultural
Description: Festival of colors welcoming spring. People throw colored powder and water at each other. Most enthusiastic celebration at Kathmandu Durbar Square and Pokhara Lakeside.
Tip: Wear old white clothes, protect your phone/camera, and join the fun!

Name: Indra Jatra
Date: September (8 days)
Type: Religious/Cultural
Description: Kathmandu's biggest street festival honoring Lord Indra. Features chariot procession of Living Goddess Kumari, masked dances, and the erection of the lingo pole at Durbar Square.
Tip: Watch the chariot procession at Kathmandu Durbar Square on the main day.

Name: Buddha Jayanti
Date: April-May (Full moon day)
Type: Religious
Description: Celebrates Buddha's birth, enlightenment, and death. Special celebrations at Boudhanath, Swayambhunath, and Lumbini. Monasteries decorated with lights and prayer ceremonies.
Tip: Visit Lumbini for the most authentic celebration experience.

Name: Teej
Date: August-September (3 days)
Type: Religious/Cultural
Description: Women's festival where married women fast and pray for their husbands' long life. Women dress in red saris, sing, dance, and visit Pashupatinath Temple.
Tip: See thousands of women in red at Pashupatinath Temple on the main day.
