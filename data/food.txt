Name: Dal Bhat
Type: Main Course
Vegetarian: Yes (can be vegan)
Description: Nepal's national dish. Steamed rice served with lentil soup (dal), vegetable curry, pickle (achar), and sometimes papad. Comes with unlimited refills.
Tip: Try "Dal Bhat Power, 24 Hour" - Nepali saying about this energy-packed meal.

Name: Momo
Type: Snack/Street Food
Vegetarian: Available
Description: Tibetan-origin dumplings filled with meat or vegetables, steamed or fried. Served with spicy tomato-based chutney. Found everywhere from street stalls to restaurants.
Tip: Try buff (buffalo) momos for authentic local flavor. Best in Kathmandu's backstreets.

Name: Thukpa
Type: Soup/Noodles
Vegetarian: Available
Description: Hearty Tibetan noodle soup with vegetables and meat. Popular in mountain regions and Kathmandu. Perfect warming dish after trekking or in winter.
Tip: Try Thenthuk (hand-pulled noodle version) for a more authentic experience.

Name: Sel Roti
Type: Snack/Breakfast
Vegetarian: Yes
Description: Ring-shaped sweet rice bread, crispy outside and soft inside. Deep-fried and eaten during festivals especially Tihar and Dashain. Popular breakfast with tea.
Tip: Best eaten fresh and hot from street vendors during festivals.

Name: Newari Khaja Set
Type: Platter
Vegetarian: No
Description: Traditional Newari snack platter with beaten rice (chiura), spiced buffalo meat, boiled egg, black soybeans, pickle, and aila (local liquor).
Tip: Visit Kirtipur or Bhaktapur for the most authentic Newari food experience.

Name: Chatamari
Type: Snack
Vegetarian: Available
Description: Newari rice flour crepe topped with minced meat or vegetables and egg. Often called "Nepali pizza". Popular festival food.
Tip: Eat it fresh off the pan at Newari restaurants in Patan.

Name: Juju Dhau
Type: Dessert
Vegetarian: Yes
Description: "King of Yogurt" from Bhaktapur. Thick, creamy sweet yogurt made from buffalo milk in traditional clay pots. Famous throughout Nepal.
Tip: The authentic version is only found in Bhaktapur. Try it near Bhaktapur Durbar Square.

Name: Yomari
Type: Dessert/Snack
Vegetarian: Yes
Description: Newari sweet dumpling made from rice flour, filled with molasses and sesame seeds. Eaten during Yomari Punhi festival in December.
Tip: Find them in Patan and Bhaktapur during winter months.
